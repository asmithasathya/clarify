\begin{abstract}
We study intent stabilization for ambiguous user requests. The core system samples multiple structured interpretations of a request, detects unstable understanding points, selectively resamples the weakest stage, and asks a clarification question only when intent uncertainty remains high. The repository now supports the full research workflow around that method: frozen train/dev/test data splits, baseline and ablation matrices, teacher rollout export, modular student distillation, optional DPO preparation, and a living manuscript whose tables are generated directly from experiment artifacts. As of April 21, 2026, the first completed development-sweep win improves InfoQuest-dev task-success rate from \texttt{0.24} for \texttt{targeted\_clarify} to \texttt{0.29} for \texttt{resample\_clarify} with matched \texttt{1.00} appropriate-action rate. This manuscript is therefore complete in structure but intentionally incomplete in some result tables: every unfinished test-set, ablation, audit, repeatability, or post-training artifact is rendered as an explicit placeholder rather than replaced by stale or incompatible numbers.
\end{abstract}
